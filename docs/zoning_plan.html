Zone Types

The world is divided into 8 distinct zone types, each with unique rules for claiming, building, trading, and visibility. Zones radiate in concentric rings from the Origin Hub at (0,0).

🏛️
Origin Hub
Public hub with pre-built showcase tracks, tutorial portals, and central marketplace. Admin-curated — no private claims.
📍 Radius: 0–3 🔒 Claim: Not allowed 👥 Build: Admin only
🏡
Starter Ring
First ring of claimable sites for new players. Low entry barrier — 10 coin claim cost, max 3 sites per player.
📍 Radius: 4–8 🪙 Claim: 10 coins 🏗️ Height limit: 40 📦 Parts limit: 300
🏘️
Suburban Ring
Mid-tier residential. Full build height, marketplace trading enabled. More terrain types unlocked.
📍 Radius: 9–15 🪙 Claim: 50 coins 🏗️ Height limit: 80 📦 Parts limit: 500
🏪
Commercial Corridor
High-traffic zones flanking transit paths. Premium terrain. Auto-listing after 7 days of inactivity.
📍 Radius: 16–20 🪙 Claim: 200 coins ⚡ Auto-list: 7 days inactive 📦 Parts limit: 500
🏭
Industrial Sector
Workshop integration zone. Higher part limits. Shared asset libraries for 3D model creation.
📍 Radius: 21–28 🪙 Claim: 100 coins 📦 Parts limit: 1000 🔧 Workshop: Enabled
🎮
Recreational Park
Public racing arenas. Community-built tracks. Voting system for track of the week. Leaderboards.
📍 Radius: 29–35 🔒 Claim: Community only 🏆 Voting: Enabled 📦 Parts limit: 800
🚧
Frontier
Unzoned expansion territory. First-come first-served. Experimental terrain types. Future content drops.
📍 Radius: 36+ 🪙 Claim: Free 🔬 Experimental: Yes 📦 Parts limit: 200
🛡️
Admin Reserve
Admin-protected areas for featured content, seasonal events, and moderation tools. Not player-claimable.
📍 Scattered special areas 🔒 Claim: Not allowed 🎄 Events: Seasonal ⚙️ Moderation: Active

Monetization & Economy

🪙 Site Economy

Claim costs scale with zone prestige. Commercial corridor sites (200 coins) are 20× more expensive than starter sites (10 coins). This creates a natural progression path — players earn coins by building popular tracks, then graduate to premium zones.

The auto-listing system (7-day inactivity in Commercial, 21-day in Suburban) prevents land hoarding and keeps the marketplace liquid. Inactive sites re-enter the economy.

📊 Network Effects (Metcalfe's Law)

Each new player adds value to ALL existing players through shared tracks, marketplace liquidity, and community voting. The concentric ring model ensures density — new players cluster near existing ones rather than sprawling into empty space.

Phase-gating zones by player count creates a shared goal — the community works together to unlock new territory.

🎯 Behavioral Economics

Loss Aversion: Auto-listing after inactivity creates urgency — players return to maintain their premium Commercial sites.

Endowed Progress: Starter sites are cheap (10 coins) but limited (3 per player). Players feel ownership early and are motivated to upgrade.

Decoy Pricing: Frontier sites are free but limited — making Suburban sites at 50 coins feel like a bargain by comparison.

🔒 Security Notes

Zone rules are computed client-side for UX responsiveness but enforced server-side via world_networking.js. The canClaimSite() and canBuildAtSite() validators run on both client and server — the server response is authoritative.

Admin zones use override functions (getZoneOverride()) that can be injected server-side for seasonal events without client updates.
